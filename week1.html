Week 1: Program Launch, Leadership Identity & Workforce Ecosystems

🎯Week 1 Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, fellows will be able to:

  1. Describe workforce development as a complex ecosystem involving education, employers, government, philanthropy, and community organizations.
  2. Explain the role of the Opportunity Accelerator (OA) framework in advancing equitable economic mobility.
  3. Identify major workforce system actors operating within their local communities.
  4. Begin developing a leadership identity grounded in systems thinking, collaboration, and equity-centered implementation.
  5. Analyze how workforce systems, labor market conditions, and community supports shape economic mobility outcomes.
  6. Create a preliminary map of the workforce ecosystem in their local community.

👋Welcome to OA Fellows Cohort 9

On behalf of Goodwill Industries International (GII), welcome to OA Fellows Cohort 9.

The OA Fellows program was created to strengthen leadership capacity across the Goodwill network and prepare workforce professionals to navigate the increasing complexity of workforce development systems.

Communities across the country are experiencing rapid labor market shifts, changing employer expectations, persistent inequities in access to opportunity, and growing pressure to demonstrate impact and return on investment in workforce programs.

In response, OA Fellows must be prepared to:

  • think systemically
  • design equitable solutions
  • build cross-sector partnerships
  • interpret labor market realities
  • implement evidence-based practices
  • and sustain organizational change

The OA Fellows experience is designed to strengthen leadership practice and implementation capacity while contributing to the broader impact of the Goodwill network.

🚀 The Fellowship is not simply a professional development program.

👉It is a leadership development experience designed to prepare fellows to influence workforce systems and improve economic mobility across the communities you serve.

📊The Opportunity Accelerator Framework

The Opportunity Accelerator (OA) framework provides Goodwill organizations with a structured approach to advancing equitable economic mobility.

The framework recognizes that workforce outcomes are influenced not only by training and employment services, but also by broader systems and conditions including:

  • educational access
  • employer practices
  • transportation
  • housing stability
  • childcare
  • labor market demand
  • technology access
  • and public policy

OA Framework Principles

The OA framework emphasizes:

  • person-centered service delivery
  • systems thinking
  • employer partnership
  • evidence-based decision-making
  • implementation fidelity
  • and long-term participant advancement
👉Throughout the Fellowship, fellows will use the OA framework not as a reference, but as a decision-making tool to assess, design, and implement solutions that advance equitable economic mobility.

👔Systems Leadership in Workforce Development

Traditional leadership often focuses on managing programs or supervising teams.

Systems leadership requires a broader perspective.

What Systems Leaders Do

Systems leaders:

  • understand how institutions interact
  • identify structural barriers
  • align resources toward shared outcomes
  • build cross-sector partnerships
  • use data to guide decision-making
  • and create conditions for sustainable change

In workforce development, systems leadership recognizes that economic mobility is shaped by multiple interconnected factors.

OA Fellows are expected to strengthen both operational expertise and systems leadership capacity throughout the Fellowship.

🟦Stepping Into Your Role as a Workforce Systems Leader

This Fellowship is not about observing or learning concepts in isolation.

It is about applying a systems perspective to your role starting now.

As you begin this experience, consider:

  • Where do you currently influence decisions?
  • Where do you want to influence decisions?
  • What opportunities exist to align your work more closely with system-level outcomes?
👉Throughout the Fellowship, you will move from understanding systems to actively shaping them.

📋OA Fellows Program Overview

Program Structure

The OA Fellows program is a cohort-based leadership experience that combines:

  • virtual learning sessions
  • applied implementation activities
  • peer collaboration
  • leadership development
  • and organizational practice integration

The program is designed to support workforce professionals in applying the Opportunity Accelerator framework with fidelity while strengthening systems leadership skills.

Throughout the Fellowship, Fellows Will:

  • engage in collaborative learning
  • strengthen leadership and change management skills
  • analyze workforce systems and labor market dynamics
  • explore evidence-based workforce practices
  • apply OA tools and frameworks
  • participate in implementation-focused activities
  • contribute to peer learning across the Goodwill network
  • and develop strategies to improve economic mobility outcomes

🔍Applied Activity: Mapping Your Local Workforce Ecosystem

Purpose

To better understand the systems, organizations, and partnerships that shape workforce outcomes and economic mobility in your community.

👉This activity will serve as a foundation for understanding how OA Service Delivery Areas are currently operating within your local workforce ecosystem.

Instructions

Create a preliminary map of your local workforce ecosystem.

Include:

Workforce & Training Partners

  • Workforce boards
  • American Job Centers
  • Adult education providers
  • Community and technical colleges
  • Occupational training providers
  • Apprenticeship programs

Employers & Industry Partners

  • Major employers
  • Industry associations
  • Chambers of commerce
  • Sector partnerships

Community & Stabilization Supports

  • Housing organizations
  • Transportation providers
  • Childcare resources
  • Mental health and recovery services
  • Community-based organizations

Government & Funding Partners

  • State and local agencies
  • Philanthropic organizations
  • Public funding streams
  • Economic development organizations

Reflection Questions

As You Complete Your Ecosystem Map

  1. Where do strong partnerships currently exist?
  2. Where are the largest gaps or breakdowns within the system?
  3. Which organizations hold the greatest influence over workforce outcomes?
  4. Which populations may still be underserved or excluded?
  5. Where does your organization create the greatest value within the ecosystem?
  6. What opportunities exist for stronger collaboration or alignment?

Deliverable

Bring your completed workforce ecosystem map to Week 2.

The goal is not perfection.

👉The goal is to begin viewing workforce development through a systems lens.
